Meet your Denmark Buddy.

Settling in is not really about the paperwork. It is the hundred small things no one tells you. A Denmark Buddy is a real person who has been here a while, matched to you, to walk a few steps of the way. Part guide, part friend, part local ambassador.

  1. 1
    Get matched

    We pair you with a buddy near you who fits your situation, your field, and your language.

  2. 2
    Meet up

    A coffee, a walk, a call. They answer the awkward questions and show you how things really work here.

  3. 3
    Explore and settle

    A tour of your neighbourhood, help with a specific problem, and a friendly face through your first months.

How it works

Four steps. No paperwork wall.

  1. 1

    Join for free

    Sign up in a few minutes. No fee, no contract. You are a member from day one.

  2. 2

    Tell us what you need

    A short check-in points you to the right service, whether that is a contract review or a coffee.

  3. 3

    Get matched

  4. 4

    Stay connected

    Come to events, give back when you can, and help the next person who arrives.

Your voice

Services help one person. Advocacy helps everyone.

Some problems cannot be solved one coffee at a time. They sit in the rules. So InDe members approved an eight point Policy Agenda, and we carry it to the institutions and policymakers who can remove the barriers for good.

Approved by InDe members, May 2026.

  • 01 A national integration strategy
  • 02 An independent body to monitor progress
  • 03 International representation in policymaking
  • 04 Fair treatment in the labour market
  • 05 Access to work for spouses and partners
  • 06 Fewer barriers and costs to renew residence
  • 07 Public services you can use in a language you read
  • 08 A real housing strategy

Good to know

Questions people ask first.

Is InDe membership free?

Yes. Anyone can join free and belong from day one. Silver and Platinum add deeper one to one support, and you start with a free call, no cost and no pressure.

Who can join?

Every international who makes Denmark home, of any nationality and in any city, and the Danes who welcome them.

What is a Denmark Buddy?

A real person who has lived here a while, matched to you, to help you settle. Part guide, part friend, part local ambassador.

What does Building now mean?

Some services are live today and some are still being set up. Each service says which, so you always know what is real right now.

How is InDe funded?

The community runs on people, not fees. Most of what InDe does is delivered by volunteers and funded by partner organisations.

Denmark is better when internationals stay.

Membership is free to start. The help is real. And you are not the only one who feels far from home. Join InDe, and the next person who arrives will not have to do it alone either.
